Adobo Chicken

Never say NO to Adobo

Adobo is a traditional Filipino dish that is loved for its savory and tangy flavors. This recipe features chicken thighs marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices, then simmered until tender. Serve it with steamed rice for a comforting meal.

Adobo chicken has been my family's go-to dish for years and I hope that you will enjoy it as much as we do.

Ingredients

  • Boneless Skinless Chicken Thighs (1.5 lbs)
  • All-Purpose Soy Sauce (1/3 Cup)
  • White Vinager (1/3 Cup)
  • Ground Black Pepper (1 Tbsp)
  • Garlic Cloves, minced (6 cloves)
  • Bay Leaves (2 leaves)
  • Olive Oil (2 Tbsp)
  • Water (3/4 Cup)
  • Green Onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the soy sauce, vinegar, black pepper, minced garlic, and bay leaves. Add the chicken thighs and marinate for at least 30 minutes, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and brown the chicken thighs on both sides, about 5 minutes per side.
  3. Once browned, pour the reserved marinade and water into the skillet. Bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and cook for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  4. Remove the lid and let the sauce reduce for another 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should thicken slightly.
  5. Serve the adobo chicken hot, garnished with chopped green onions. Enjoy it with steamed rice or your favorite side dish.
